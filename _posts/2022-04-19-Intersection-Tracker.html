Earlier this year, Mayor Eric Adams and DOT Commissioner Ydanis Rodriguez promised to upgrade 1,000 intersections across New York City for safety. These improvements could include installing raised crosswalks, leading pedestrian intervals, shortening crossings, or other interventions.

The city has not comprehensively published where intersection upgrades have taken place or what metrics are being used to determine which intersections get improvements, so Transportation Alternatives's Brooklyn Activist Committee is tracking the administration's commitment to meeting this goal.
